Intro

We had the opportunity to work with AutoCAD and AutoDesk on a number of projects throughout the course of my employment at Lightboard. Our internal team provided a wide range of marketing collateral for the folks over in the San Francisco office, ranging from custom illustration, social media ads as well as a variety of other internal communication collateral.

Background

Our network of designers put together some great work and even implemented an effective visual style for the brand moving forward. During this time, I was able to work on some great projects, providing a range of custom illustrations to be displayed on the Autodesk blog featuring articles pertaining to the industry leading 2D / 3D CAD software.

During initial illustration explorations, images ranged in visual styles to fit the mood and direction for each, specific blog article.

Blog Article 1

One of the earlier projects was an illustrated series of blog images pertaining to 5 tools of innovation. Our main goal was to execute a hand-drawn, loose aesthetic with colors and style to match the provided reference images.

Blog Article 3: Sabremetrics

This blog post was a personal favorite. We were looking to personify the specific software for a guest post on the Autodesk blog. This was relating to grabbing quality data and gauging compiled metrics. For an interesting visual approach, we decided to personify several baseball-related scenarios in which data could be captured, witnessed or analyzed.

Our initial sketches offered some loose visual concepts we could carry over into a final product. While the concepts functioned successfully, they were over the top in detail. We put together a new set of sketches, eventually locking down accurate renderings prior to vector illustration.
